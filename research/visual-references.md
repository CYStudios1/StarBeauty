## Concept 01: Editorial Premium

**Vibe:** Magazine-worthy. A beauty supply chain that looks like it belongs in Architectural Digest. Sharp, confident, sophisticated -- but warm enough that a customer on 87th Street feels respected, not alienated. Think Aesop meets a Black-owned hair salon with taste.

### Hero Imagery Anchors (Unsplash)

1. **https://unsplash.com/photos/J1OScm_uHUQ** -- Prince Akachi
   Black woman with natural hair against neutral background. Strong, editorial portrait energy. Clean composition for text overlay on one side (asymmetric hero).

2. **https://unsplash.com/photos/f6HbVnGtNnY** -- Leon ellDOT
   Woman in black shirt against deep red wall. The color contrast is bold but editorial. Works as a dominant visual with typography layered adjacent.

3. **https://unsplash.com/photos/SaRYwQMSVzA** -- (Unsplash editorial)
   Beauty portrait with afro hairstyle. Clean studio lighting, warm tones. Editorial without feeling sterile.

### Typography (Google Fonts)

1. **Fraunces** -- 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold) -- Display/headline font. Variable optical sizing. An old-style soft serif with character -- NOT a generic Playfair clone. The wonky terminals give it personality.
2. **DM Sans** -- 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 700 (Bold) -- Body/UI font. Clean geometric sans with slightly humanist proportions. More refined than Inter, less sterile than Helvetica.
3. **Instrument Serif** -- 400 (Regular) -- Pull quotes, accent moments. An elegant editorial serif with beautiful italics.
4. **Space Grotesk** is BANNED for this concept. Too obvious, too "AI-coded."
5. **Libre Caslon Display** -- 400 -- Alternate display option if Fraunces feels too playful. More traditional editorial weight.

### Reference Site

**byredo.com** -- Captures the editorial restraint and gallery-like pacing this concept needs. The way Byredo treats product imagery as art objects (not commerce items) and uses generous negative space as a deliberate design element -- that's the energy. But Star Beauty's version must be warmer. The Byredo ice needs to melt into something that still says "we know you by name."

### Three Forbidden Defaults

1. **No `rounded-2xl` on cards.** This concept is sharp corners only -- `0px` or `2px` max. Rounding is casual; this concept is not.
2. **No full-width hero CTA buttons.** CTAs should be text links or slim, understated buttons. The editorial tone doesn't shout.
3. **No centered-everything layout.** This concept REQUIRES left-aligned body text and asymmetric compositions. Centered = generic.

## Concept 02: Vibrant Modern

**Vibe:** Bold, energetic, unapologetically Black. The beauty supply store that shows up on your TikTok feed and makes you drive across town. Color-confident, motion-rich, contemporary. Think The Lip Bar energy meets a modern e-commerce experience with actual personality.

### Hero Imagery Anchors (Unsplash)

1. **https://unsplash.com/photos/QS9ZX5UnS14** -- Jessica Felicio
   Woman with braids against a yellow-painted wall. The warm background color creates energy. Perfect for vibrant concept -- color already built into the image.

2. **https://unsplash.com/photos/iIvTbiXr2Kc** -- Edu Bastidas
   Woman with purple shirt against pink background. Bold color blocking -- the kind of image that anchors a vibrant hero without needing artificial gradients.

3. **https://unsplash.com/photos/xmSWVeGEnJw** -- Good Faces
   Woman with afro looking directly at camera. Confident, magnetic. The direct gaze makes this a statement hero, not decorative.

### Typography (Google Fonts)

1. **Sora** -- 400 (Regular), 600 (SemiBold), 800 (ExtraBold) -- Headline font. Geometric sans with just enough softness. Wide letterforms feel contemporary and confident.
2. **Outfit** -- 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 600 (SemiBold) -- Body font. Clean, modern, geometric. Excellent readability at body sizes.
3. **Anybody** -- 400, 900 (Black) -- Display accents. Variable width font -- can go ultra-condensed for typographic moments. Brings ENERGY.
4. **Bricolage Grotesque** -- 400, 700 -- Alternate headline option. Quirky proportions that feel handmade without being illegible.
5. **Cabinet Grotesk** -- Available on Google Fonts as alternative. Strong, confident grotesque.

### Reference Site

**thelipbar.com** -- Captures the bold, Black-owned beauty energy this concept channels. The way TLB uses vibrant color as PUNCTUATION (not wallpaper), combines UGC video with product photography, and leads with benefit badges that show values rather than stating them. Star Beauty's version should feel even more Chicago -- less DTC startup, more neighborhood institution with a fresh coat of paint.

### Three Forbidden Defaults

1. **No muted/pastel palette.** This concept demands saturated color. No `#F5F5F5` gray cards. If it's gray, it's wrong.
2. **No identical card grids.** Product sections must break the grid -- varied sizes, overlapping elements, or asymmetric arrangements.
3. **No subtle hover states.** Hovers in this concept should POP -- color shifts, scale transforms, shadow reveals. Opacity changes are banned.

## Concept 03: Neighborhood Trusted

**Vibe:** Warm, familiar, welcoming. The beauty supply store that's been on your block since you were getting your hair done with your mama. Community-first, trust-forward, like coming home. Not trying to be Sephora -- proudly local, authentically Chicago. Think the best local business you've ever patronized, with a website that matches the feeling of walking through the door.

### Hero Imagery Anchors (Unsplash)

1. **https://unsplash.com/photos/x89llgw6qg8** -- Danielle Bailey
   Woman sitting in grass with a genuine smile. Natural, warm, approachable. This is the "come as you are" energy of the neighborhood concept.

2. **https://unsplash.com/photos/h1lA3N5wb8M** -- Divine Effiong
   Woman with flower on ear. Warm, natural styling that feels personal and intimate rather than editorial or commercial.

3. **https://unsplash.com/photos/W6Qt8mUL4L0** -- Thandy Yung
   Woman in red dress in a field. Warm, grounded, real. Not a studio shot -- feels like someone you'd actually know.

### Typography (Google Fonts)

1. **Source Serif 4** -- 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold) -- Headline font. A workhorse serif that's warm without being precious. More approachable than Fraunces, more interesting than Georgia.
2. **Nunito Sans** -- 400 (Regular), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold) -- Body/UI font. Rounded terminals make it inherently friendly. Clean and readable without being clinical.
3. **Lora** -- 400 (Regular), 400 Italic, 700 (Bold) -- Pull quotes, accent text. A transitional serif with calligraphic warmth. Beautiful italics.
4. **Atkinson Hyperlegible Next** -- 400, 700 -- Alternate body option. Designed for maximum readability -- community accessibility as a design value.
5. **Castoro** -- 400, 400 Italic -- Alternative display serif. Warm, readable, with personality.

### Reference Site

**patternbeauty.com** -- Captures the warm, community-centered beauty brand energy this concept needs. The way Pattern uses texture-based navigation (honoring customer identity), carousel-based product storytelling, and warm brand yellow as a structural color -- that's the warmth level. Star Beauty's version should feel even more grounded in place -- the Chicago locations, the 29-year history, the specific products at specific prices.

### Three Forbidden Defaults

1. **No pure black text on pure white.** This concept uses warm tones -- off-white backgrounds (`#FDF8F3` range), dark brown text (`#2C1810` range). Pure black/white is too stark.
2. **No sharp corners.** Border radius is `8px`--`12px` everywhere. This concept is soft, warm, approachable.
3. **No "Shop Now" CTAs.** Buttons in this concept use specific, warm language: "Find your store," "See what's on sale," "Come say hi." Generic commerce language is banned.
